A redesign exploration
Keep the player inside the story.

The tools are all here — five verbs, dozens of oracles. But the solo loop (set scene → roll → interpret → record → advance) is scattered across them. This canvas proposes a faster, calmer, more guided experience: fewer trips, clearer state, one-tap everything — and a warmer, more literary skin.

Five principles
01
Stay in the journal
Roll, interpret and record without ever leaving the story surface.
02
Always answer "where am I?"
Scene, chaos, threads and progress summarized at a glance.
03
Speed over depth
Quick actions, a command palette, one-tap rolls and shortcuts.
04
Earn every pixel
Cut HUD noise; raise the signal of the entries that carry decisions.
05
A little delight
Literary type, tactile paper cards, gentle motion as reward.

Let the AI introduce itself

On-device AI is a headline feature hidden in Settings. A contextual nudge appears the first time you'd benefit (e.g. after a juicy roll), the download is framed by value, and a consistent ✦ glyph marks every AI action so the capability is legible everywhere.

Why it helps
  • Shown once, dismissible, never nags
  • Value-first framing beats a settings toggle
  • Off by default + on-device messaging preserved

A journal that feels like a tome

The bold direction leans into the solo-journaling fantasy: subtle paper-grain, a literary Newsreader serif for narrative and oracle answers, an illuminated drop cap at each scene, warmer card gradients, and a single floating ✦ as the AI motif. The crisp Hanken Grotesk sans keeps controls and data honest.

The treats
  • Paper-grain surface + soft sienna shadows
  • Serif headlines & oracle answers; sans for UI
  • Drop-cap scene openers; hairline dividers
  • Gentle motion only as reward (✦, roll settle)
PRINCIPLE Delight that respects reduced-motion & keeps data legible
