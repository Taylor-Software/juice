# Stranger-Test Kit — run 5 real first sessions

Wedge Phase 4 calls for five people who aren't the author to each play one
full solo session while you watch where they stall. This kit makes each
session repeatable and comparable. Budget ~45 minutes per person.

## Who to recruit (aim for a spread)

1. Someone who has never played a TTRPG (video-game RPGs fine).
2. A group-play D&D player who has never soloed.
3. An experienced solo player (Mythic / Ironsworn / journaling games).
4. Anyone from an actual-play audience (watches, never played).
5. Wildcard — whoever you can get.

## Setup (before they arrive)

- Fresh install or wiped prefs (welcome card + AI offer must fire).
- Screen recording on. You sit beside them; you may not touch or point.
- The only prompt you give: **"This app lets you play a tabletop RPG
  by yourself. Play for half an hour. Think out loud."**

## The one rule for you

**Silence.** Answer nothing. If they ask "what do I do?", say "whatever you'd
do at home." Every question they ask you IS a finding — write it down verbatim.

## Tasks (don't hand these over; check them off as they happen naturally)

- [ ] Gets past the welcome card + AI offer without confusion
- [ ] Creates a campaign (note every wizard hesitation, chip hover, backtrack)
- [ ] Understands, in their own words, who narrates (ask at debrief, not before)
- [ ] Starts a scene
- [ ] Asks the oracle a question — with the question stated/typed, not a blind roll
- [ ] Writes at least one line of prose in the journal
- [ ] Interprets one oracle answer into fiction (any medium: typed, spoken)
- [ ] Finds the Track surface (threads or characters) unprompted
- [ ] Returns to the loop for a second beat without help
- [ ] Session ends with them able to say what would happen next

## Observation sheet (one per person)

| Minute | Screen | What they did | What they said | Stall? (none/friction/hard) |
|--------|--------|---------------|----------------|------------------------------|

Log every stall ≥10 seconds of visible confusion. Note the exact words on
screen at that moment.

## Debrief (5 minutes, after — not during)

1. "In one sentence: what is this app?"
2. "Who was telling the story?"
3. "What did the [Yes But] answer mean to you?"
4. "What almost made you quit?"
5. "What would you need to play again tomorrow without me here?"
6. Features they never touched (check: Map, Run, Sheet, cards, timers) —
   candidates for the roadmap kill-list.

## Scoring

A feature is **validated** when ≥3 of 5 reach it unprompted and can explain it
at debrief. A stall is **release-blocking** when ≥2 of 5 hit it hard. Compare
against `docs/superpowers/audits/2026-07-07-stranger-test-audit.md` — the
simulated run predicted S1–S10; the humans confirm, refute, and extend.
