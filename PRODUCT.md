# Product

## Platform

web

One Flutter codebase ships web, iOS, Android, and desktop with a single
Material-based design language — it does not swap design language per OS, so it
is authored as `web`. The phone viewport is a first-class constraint, not a
scaled-down desktop: design decisions must hold at 375px width alongside
desktop web. Web (https://taylor-software.github.io/juice/) is the front door.

## Users

**Primary: tabletop RPG players who have never played without a GM.** They know
what a d20 and a character sheet are; they do not know what an oracle is, what a
chaos factor does, or what to do after the oracle says "No, but." They arrive
curious and can be lost in one confusing screen.

**Also served: experienced solo players** who already run Mythic/Ironsworn/Juice
loops and want depth, system coverage, and speed. Their depth stays reachable
but must never front the surface ahead of a newcomer's next move.

**Situation:** one person, alone, playing a roleplaying game — at a desk, on a
couch, or on a phone at a table. No other humans in the loop. Sessions are
self-paced and resumable across days.

**Job:** keep a story moving and end up with a record of it, without a game
master.

## Product Purpose

Run the guided solo-play loop: **set a scene → ask the oracle → read the answer
in fiction → journal the beat**, repeated. The app supplies the missing GM's
two functions — an impartial answer source and a memory — while the player
supplies the fiction.

Success in a session, in the order design must optimize:

1. **The player is never blocked by not knowing what's next.** Blank-page
   paralysis is the failure mode that ends solo campaigns.
2. **Answers are fast at the table.** Question to oracle answer in as few taps
   as possible, from wherever the player already is.
3. **The player stays in flow.** Nothing forces a detour into tooling or breaks
   fiction; session length is the signal.
4. **The journal is worth rereading.** The exported story is the artifact the
   session was for.

## Positioning

Not a dice roller with a notes field, and not a rules database. Its
differentiator is the **loop as a first-class surface**: the oracle, the
generators, the trackers, the maps, and the AI readings all deposit into one
campaign journal, and every rolled result can be read back in-fiction at the
point of the roll rather than in a separate tool.

Two more claims a neighboring product cannot truthfully copy without doing the
same work:

- **Verified table data.** Every oracle/generator table is generated by a
  `build_*.py` script that self-verifies dice-range coverage and probabilities
  against the source. The script is the source of truth; the JSON asset is
  emitted, never hand-edited.
- **Private by construction.** No account, no server, no telemetry. Campaigns
  live on the device. The optional AI runs on-device (with one opt-in cloud
  seam on the user's own key). Nothing the player writes leaves the device
  unless they export a file themselves.

## Operating Context

- **Six verbs** in one shell: Journal (home), Sheet, Ask, Map, Track, Run.
  The Journal is where play lives; everything else feeds it.
- **Campaigns** are the top-level container. A campaign enables a set of
  *systems* (rulesets, oracles, exploration toolkits, tools), and the enabled
  set gates which surfaces, subtabs, slash commands, and header chips exist.
  A player sees only their game's tools.
- **Multi-session, resumable.** Play is picked up days later; a "Previously
  on…" resume card and per-campaign persistence carry the thread.
- **Input modes are plural on purpose:** typed prose, slash commands (`/`),
  mentions (`@`), sketches, imported images and annotated PDF pages, and
  one-tap rolls from the header, the composer, and the loop bar.
- **Sharing is file-based:** Markdown, story-prose, and standalone HTML
  exports; `.juice.json` / `.juice.zip` campaign files; `.tables.json` table
  packs and oracle packs. Cloud sync is BYO (save into a synced folder).

## Capabilities and Constraints

**Confirmed capabilities**

- Oracles: Juice (Fate Check), Mythic GME 2e (Fate Chart, Scene Test, 47 Meaning
  Tables), Roll High, card/tarot decks with spreads, and a player-built
  **oracle constructor** (computed ranges from any dice notation).
- Generators: 28 registered, plus user-authored **custom random tables**
  (uniform / weighted / range modes) and a bundled starter pack.
- ~20 character sheets: system-agnostic plus bespoke sheets for the Ironsworn
  family, D&D 5e, Shadowdark, Nimble, Draw Steel, Argosa, Cairn, Knave 2e,
  OSE/B/X, Embark 2E, Kal-Arath, DCC, a generalized 0-level funnel, and a
  schema-driven **Custom/Homebrew** builder.
- Trackers: threads (with progress clocks and success tallies), scenes, rumors,
  people, places, encounters with initiative and stat blocks, a bestiary.
- Maps: dungeon (including the Roll 4 Ruin classic generator with caves and
  multi-level), wilderness hexcrawl, Verdant Journey, settlements — all
  snapshot-able into the journal.
- Content library: SRD 5.1 and 5.2 spells and monsters, plus monsters/spells
  for the free-licensed systems; per-system Rules QuickRef cards and
  user-authored reference cards.
- Optional AI (opt-in, off by default): oracle interpretation, GM chat,
  narration, flesh-out, character voice, recap, and LLM-ranked suggestions.

**Durable constraints**

- **Licensing posture governs what content can exist.** Vendoring rulebook
  content plus required attribution is allowed only when the license is
  free/open (CC-BY, CC-BY-SA, OGL, open creator licenses). Systems without such
  a license (Shadowdark, Kal-Arath) ship *facts-only*: mechanics and procedures,
  no rulebook prose, no attribution, no "compatible with" claim. The app is
  **free and non-commercial** because several sources are CC BY-**NC**.
  `THIRD_PARTY.md` is the canonical inventory.
- **Generated data is generated.** Table assets come from `build_*.py` rails
  that self-verify; never hand-edit the emitted JSON.
- **Local-first persistence.** SharedPreferences keyed per campaign; no backend.
  Some state is deliberately app-global and un-exported (bestiary, custom
  tables, constructed oracles, reference cards, AI enable flag).
- **Lean stack, few rails.** Riverpod plus shared_preferences plus a short list
  of packages; no router, no codegen, no repository layer. Add a rail only when
  a real need appears.
- **The on-device model is a large opt-in download** (~3.7 GB, Gemma 4 E4B);
  the on-device LLM is disabled entirely on web. Every AI affordance is hidden
  unless the model is present *and* explicitly enabled, so **every screen must
  be complete and good with AI off**.
- **Dice stay authoritative.** The model suggests; it never adjudicates.
- **Solo only.** GM/party-session mode was deliberately removed. Every entry
  point lands on the Journal.
- Pre-release: backward compatibility with legacy campaign data is deliberately
  not maintained.

**Terminology (product vocabulary, use it exactly)**

Campaign · scene · chaos factor · thread · tally · oracle · Fate Check ·
verb (Journal / Sheet / Ask / Map / Track / Run) · the loop · reading ·
Inspire · system · ruleset · funnel · quick-roll · HUD.

## Brand Commitments

- **Product name: "Solo Adventurer's Journal."** This is the display name in
  all in-app titles, platform manifests, web title/manifest, and the README H1.
- **"Juice" is not the product name and must never be renamed away where it is
  correct:** the Juice *oracle system* by jrruethe that the app implements
  (`'juice'` oracle id, `fate-juice`, the Juice-oracle opt-in and badge), and
  the internal identifiers kept for stability (the `juice_oracle` Dart package,
  `juice.*` preference keys, the `juice-oracle` campaign-file marker,
  `.juice.json` / `.juice.zip`, and the `net.taylorsoftware.juice` bundle id).
- **Voice:** in service of the player's fiction, not over it. The app narrates
  procedure, never the story; the player writes the story.
- No confirmed logo system, color story, or type commitment is recorded here —
  visual authority is not init's to declare.

## Product Principles

1. **The next move is always visible.** A player who does not know what to do
   next has been failed by the design, not by their imagination.
2. **The journal is the product.** Every tool is a tributary; anything that
   produces a beat must be able to deposit it into the journal in one gesture,
   in one entry.
3. **The enabled systems define the surface.** Never show a player a tool for a
   game they are not playing; complexity is opt-in, and the default campaign is
   small.
4. **Facts over prose, license first.** What ships is bounded by what the
   license permits. When in doubt, ship the mechanic and not the words.
5. **Nothing leaves the device.** Privacy is a structural property, not a
   setting — no account, no server, no telemetry, and AI stays optional.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- A user-controlled **reading text scale** applies app-wide via
  `MaterialApp.builder`; layouts must survive it.
- Muted color tokens have had a **WCAG contrast pass**; new tokens must hold
  the same bar.
- Motion honors **reduced motion** (`MediaQuery.disableAnimations`) — the dice
  tumble and other animations resolve instantly when it is set.
- **375px phone width is a hard floor**, with overflow guards; a keyboard-open
  phone journal must stay writable.
- Keyboard shortcuts exist on desktop (search, quick roll, send, new scene,
  back).
- No formal conformance target (e.g. WCAG 2.2 AA) has been committed — open
  decision.
